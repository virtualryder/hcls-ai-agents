# ROI Analysis — Regulatory Writing Agent (illustrative)

Figures are modeling assumptions for a mid-size sponsor; validate against the
customer's own baselines during the assessment. They are not guarantees.

## Where the time goes today

A regulatory medical writer spends the majority of authoring time on non-writing
work: locating guidance, assembling source evidence, reconciling figures across
documents, and formatting — not reasoning about benefit-risk.

## Modeled impact

| Workflow | Before | After | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| First draft, benefit-risk summary | ~24 hrs | ~6 hrs | Evidence auto-assembled; grounded draft generated |
| Day-120 question response | ~16 hrs | ~5 hrs | Source-bounded drafting + consistency checks |
| Cross-document figure reconciliation | manual, late | automated, pre-review | Grounding verification |
| Regulatory intelligence triage | days of lag | continuous | Obligation/guidance monitoring |

## Illustrative annual value (sponsor authoring ~120 major sections/year)

| Driver | Estimate |
|---|---|
| Writer hours saved (18 hrs × 120) | ~2,160 hrs |
| Fully-loaded value (@ $120/hr) | ~$260K/yr |
| Faster submissions (cycle-time reduction) | strategic upside (earlier approval/revenue) |
| Fewer post-submission deficiencies | reduced review cycles, lower risk |

## What we measure in a pilot

Baseline vs. agent on: hours-to-first-draft, reviewer edit volume, grounding
defects caught pre-review, and cycle time. The assessment instruments these so
the business case is the customer's own data, not a generic slide.
