# HCLS AI Readiness Assessment
### Consulting Offering — Scope, Deliverables, and Engagement Structure

---

## Offering Summary

A structured four-to-six week engagement that produces an AI readiness and prioritization blueprint for a life-sciences organization considering deployment of the HCLS AI Agent Suite. The assessment evaluates the customer's current state across five dimensions — business priority, data and integration readiness, compliance posture, platform maturity, and organizational change capacity — and delivers a prioritized roadmap with a business case for the top two or three agent deployments.

This offering is the recommended entry point for customers who do not yet have a specific use case identified, or who have multiple competing use cases and need an objective prioritization framework.

---

## Scope

### In scope
- Discovery workshops with functional stakeholders: Regulatory Affairs, Pharmacovigilance / Drug Safety, Clinical Operations, Quality / QA, Medical Affairs, IT / Platform Engineering, CISO / Security, Chief Privacy Officer / DPO
- Current-state assessment of relevant systems of record (Veeva Vault RIM/Safety/Quality, Argus, Medidata, TrackWise, EDC, CTMS, eTMF) — integration API availability, data quality, and access patterns
- Compliance posture review: 21 CFR Part 11, HIPAA, GxP, GVP/E2B, FDA QMSR, EU AI Act (where applicable)
- AWS platform maturity review: Bedrock availability and account configuration, IdP federation readiness, network posture
- Fit-gap analysis against the eight agents in the suite
- Business case development for prioritized agents: ROI model, effort estimate, risk factors

### Out of scope
- Agent deployment or configuration
- Computer-system validation
- Live connector development
- Penetration testing

---

## Duration and Team

**Typical duration:** 4–6 weeks

**SI team composition:**
- 1 Senior Solution Architect (AWS / life-sciences AI)
- 1 Life Sciences Subject Matter Expert (regulatory, PV, or quality — matched to customer's priority function)
- 1 Security / Compliance Architect
- 0.5 Project Manager

**Customer time commitment:** approximately 60–80 hours of stakeholder time across the engagement, including workshops, document reviews, and briefing sessions.

---

## Deliverables

1. **HCLS AI Readiness Scorecard** — a structured assessment across five dimensions with heat-map visualization; suitable for board or executive committee presentation
2. **Agent Prioritization Matrix** — ranked view of all eight agents against the customer's specific business drivers, system readiness, and risk appetite
3. **Architecture Blueprint** — a customer-specific adaptation of the six-layer suite architecture with AWS service selections, IdP integration approach, and network topology
4. **Compliance Gap Analysis** — assessment of the customer's current control posture against the requirements for each prioritized agent's regulated context of use; identifies the validation scope for a subsequent pilot
5. **Business Case Document** — quantified ROI model for the top two agents (illustrative ranges based on process metrics gathered in discovery; customer-specific data used where available)
6. **Prioritized Roadmap** — 18-month agent deployment sequence with engagement phases, go/no-go gates, and resourcing model

---

## Entry Criteria

- Executive sponsor identified with authority over both IT and the relevant functional department(s)
- Customer willing to engage functional stakeholders (not only IT) in discovery workshops
- AWS account available or in procurement (assessment can proceed without an active account; deployment planning requires one)

## Exit Criteria

- All six deliverables reviewed and accepted by the customer's sponsor
- At least one agent deployment prioritized with a defined business case and executive support
- Go/no-go decision on proceeding to POC or Pilot offering

---

## Pricing Approach

Fixed-fee engagement based on scope. Pricing varies with customer complexity (number of priority agents assessed, number of systems of record in scope, number of regulatory jurisdictions). Typical range is structured as a single fixed-fee engagement with a scope change protocol. Expenses (travel, if applicable) billed separately at cost.

The assessment fee is typically credited toward the subsequent POC or Pilot engagement when the customer proceeds within 90 days.

---

## Connection to the Suite

The assessment is architecture-neutral — it evaluates fitness for this suite but can also identify gaps that would affect any AI deployment. When the customer proceeds to a POC or Pilot, the SI team has a structured foundation (system map, compliance posture, IdP configuration details, stakeholder alignment) that materially reduces the POC setup time.

The readiness scorecard becomes a recurring reference document: teams revisit it at each agent deployment milestone to confirm that readiness assumptions have been validated.
